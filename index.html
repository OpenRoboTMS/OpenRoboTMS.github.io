OpenRoboTMS: An Open-Source Robotic Platform for Navigated Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation

Wenzhi Bai, Andrew Weightman, Zhenhong Li†
The University of Manchester †Corresponding Author: zhenhong.li@manchester.ac.uk
SlicerRoboTMS ROSRoboTMS

Abstract

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) is a non-invasive brain stimulation technique widely used in neuroscience research and clinical applications. However, the precision and effectiveness of TMS treatments can be limited by the lack of accurate targeting and real-time feedback. To address these challenges, we present OpenRoboTMS, an open-source robotic platform designed for navigated TMS applications. OpenRoboTMS integrates advanced robotic control with real-time neuroimaging feedback to enhance the accuracy and efficacy of TMS treatments. The platform features a user-friendly interface, customizable stimulation protocols, and compatibility with various neuroimaging modalities. We demonstrate the capabilities of OpenRoboTMS through a series of experiments, showcasing its potential to improve TMS targeting and facilitate new research avenues in neuroscience and neuromodulation. The code is organising and the papers are under review, and we will release the code and the paper once they are accepted. We hope that OpenRoboTMS will serve as a valuable resource for researchers and clinicians in the field of TMS, fostering collaboration and innovation in brain stimulation technologies.

Hardware Configurations

Overview of the proposed OpenRoboTMS hardware configuration.

The figure illustrates the proposed OpenRoboTMS hardware configuration. The system consists of a robotic arm with multiple degrees of freedom, a TMS device mounted on the robotic platform, and a control system that integrates real-time neuroimaging feedback. The robotic arm enables precise positioning and orientation of the TMS device relative to the target brain region, while the control system manages the stimulation parameters and feedback loop.

Introduction Video

This video presents an overview of the proposed OpenRoboTMS platform, highlighting its background, motivations, and key features for robot-assisted Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS). It demonstrates how clinicians can perform precise stimulation through an integrated workflow encompassing calibration, MRI-to-phantom registration, target selection, and continuous robotic tracking. Built on 3D Slicer and ROS, the platform delivers an open and extensible framework that integrates hardware, software, and core algorithms, supporting reproducible research and accelerating innovation in robot-assisted TMS.
